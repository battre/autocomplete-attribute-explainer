Brazil relies heavily on structured addresses. Basically every form has separate fields for street name, house number and often an complement (for apartments, blocks, ...). Oftentimes, a lot of information is derived from the CEP (postal code).

Missing field types

The following cases are currently missing in the autocomplete spec and occurred on multiple sites:
  • street-name (endereço)
  • house-number (número)
  • additional information (Informações adicionais; Complemento (opcional) (Apartamento, sala, conjunto, edifício, andar, etc.); Complemento; Complemento e referência (Opcional); Complemento (Nº do apartamento, Bloco, etc.); Complemento (Apto/Bloco/Casa))
    Informationen going into this are:
    • apartment, room, suite
    • building, block, house - often times people live in gated communities where the entire community has the same street name and house number. The Bloco identifies the building within the community.
    • floor
    Note that the sites I investigated agreed on the use of an "additional information" field and never split the the apartment number and house name into individual fields.
  • reference-point (landmark?) (Referência; Complemento e referência; Ponto de referência) - e.g. people would put 'same street as Church xyz' there to help the delivery person find the location.

Open questions

  • I have modeled the additional information as "address-overflow" and reference-point as "landmark". Any concerns about this? This will require some localization in the UI because Brasilians probably don't think of landmarks as reference-points. Instead of "address-overflow", we could also choose "building-and-location-in-building".
