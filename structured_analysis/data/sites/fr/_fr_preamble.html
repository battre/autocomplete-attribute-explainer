French websites often work well with an address-line1 and 2. The websites may specify what to put into each line but it looks like address-line1 always gets the street name and house number.

Some websites ask for a street name and house number and then also have overflow fields.

Many websites asked for a (binary) gender.

Missing field types

The following cases are currently missing in the autocomplete spec and occurred on multiple sites:
  • street name (Numéro)
  • house number (Voie)
  • building (Bâtiment)
  • floor (Étage)
  • stair case (Escalier) - just once in the sample
  • door (Porte) - just once in the sample
  • academic title (Titel)

Open questions

  • Do we want to use lieu-dit for address-level3? Some cities have multiple sub-cities. A lieu-dit is the name of a subdivision of the city, usually very small (2-3 houses). Typically you would would put the lieu-dit into the street-address/address-lineX field because you don’t have street/house numbers in a lieu-dit.
  • Do we want to support door codes?
